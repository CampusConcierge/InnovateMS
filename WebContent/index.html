Innovate @ Olemiss

Southern Solutions to Global Problems - February 23rd - 25th 2018

Welcome Note

Innovate @ Olemiss will be the first ever Innovation hackathon at the University of Mississippi. Over the years we have seen innovative ideas come to life on several campuses across the United States from Microsoft and Facebook at Harvard to Snapchat and Google at Stanford. We have also seen non technical start ups like everquote insurance spring up an MIT. Non of these innovations have stemmed from the South, mainly due to the fact that we have historically not created an environment for innovation to Thrive. At Innovate @ Olemiss, we will be redefining "hackathons" to extend beyond the typical programmers haven for innovation by creating an inclusive environment where sharp business minds, design thinkers, hardware engineers, gifted artists and computer geniuses can come together from all over the south to develop the building blocks for a better global future.



Here is a brief snippet of what to expect.....

  • Quick Pitch Competitions and Team Forming Workshops

    In the months leading to the hackathon we will be hosting several quick pitch competitions and team forming workshops in multiple colleges to help students hone the skills that would be necessary for them to succeed at I @ O

  • Career Coaching and Job Interviews

    In the weeks leading to I @ O we will be partnering with the career development centers of participating Universities to host career coaching workshops and interview mastery workshops to better prepare students for the massive career fair that would be taking place at I @ O. This career fair will give our corporate sponsors an opportunity to recruit some of the smartness minds in the country.

  • Grand Innovation Hack and Sponsor Challenges

    Apart from the free food which always comes in handy at a hackathon, The main event will begin on Saturday February 24th 2017. All the teams would be able to finally start working on transforming their big idea into real life solutions. In addition to the grand challenge of having the best hack/idea implemented, several of our corporate sponsors would also be providing extra prizes to students bold enough to take on some additional challenges.
